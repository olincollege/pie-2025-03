Mechanical System

Overview of the mechanical design, components, and engineering decisions that enable our CNC cake froster to operate efficiently and precisely.

System Overview

The mechanical system consists of a three-axis CNC platform designed to move a frosting nozzle in X, Y, and Z directions with precision. The frame is constructed from aluminum extrusion for rigidity while maintaining a lightweight profile.

Figure 1: Complete mechanical assembly

Frame Design

Material Selection

We chose 2020 aluminum extrusion for the frame due to its:

  • High strength-to-weight ratio
  • Easy assembly with T-slot nuts and brackets
  • Modular design allowing for future modifications
  • Cost-effectiveness compared to custom machined parts

Structural Considerations

The base measures 600mm × 600mm, providing stability for the working area of 400mm × 400mm. Corner brackets and cross-bracing prevent flex during high-speed movements.

Motion System

Linear Motion

Each axis uses linear guide rails paired with lead screws for precise positioning. NEMA 17 stepper motors drive the lead screws through flexible couplers to accommodate minor misalignments.

Specifications

  • X-axis travel: 400mm
  • Y-axis travel: 400mm
  • Z-axis travel: 150mm
  • Resolution: 0.05mm per step
  • Maximum speed: 100mm/s

Figure 2: Linear rail and lead screw assembly

Frosting Delivery System

The frosting is dispensed through a pneumatically-actuated syringe system mounted on the Z-axis carriage. Air pressure is regulated to ensure consistent flow rates across different frosting viscosities.

Key Components

  • 60mL syringe reservoir
  • Solenoid valve for on/off control
  • Pressure regulator (0-30 PSI)
  • Stainless steel nozzle (2mm diameter)

Challenges and Solutions

Challenge: Initial design had excessive vibration during rapid movements.

Solution: Added damping feet under the frame and implemented acceleration limiting in the motion control software.

Challenge: Lead screw backlash caused positioning errors.

Solution: Upgraded to anti-backlash nuts and implemented software compensation for remaining play.
